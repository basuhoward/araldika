Heraldry Basics

What is heraldry?

Heraldry is the practice of designing, displaying, describing, and recording coats of arms and heraldic badges. It originated in medieval Europe and has since evolved into a tradition that represents familial, institutional, and national identities. Heraldic symbols are used to convey lineage, achievements, and values.

What is the significance of the Maltese Cross?

The Maltese Cross is an eight-pointed cross that is deeply associated with the Knights Hospitaller, who ruled Malta from 1530 to 1798. The eight points of the cross represent the eight obligations or aspirations of the Knights. Today, the Maltese Cross is a symbol of Malta's rich history and cultural heritage.

What are mottoes, and how are they used in heraldry?

Mottoes are phrases or maxims that often appear on coats of arms, typically placed below the shield. They are intended to express the values, principles, or aspirations of the individual, family, or institution represented by the arms. Our directory of mottoes on Araldika provides a list of these phrases, along with their meanings and origins.

Maltese Heraldry

What is the role of the Chief Herald of Malta?

The Chief Herald of Malta is responsible for overseeing and regulating heraldic practices in Malta. Established in 2019, the Office of the Chief Herald grants new coats of arms, registers existing ones, and promotes heraldic research and education. The Chief Herald ensures that Malta's heraldic traditions are preserved and respected.

What is a general armory?

A general armory is a comprehensive collection or registry of coats of arms, typically organized by country or region. At Araldika, our general armory features a curated selection of Maltese coats of arms, complete with detailed descriptions, blazoning, and historical context. It serves as a valuable resource for anyone interested in Maltese heraldry.

What is an ordinary of arms and an ordinary of crests?

An ordinary of arms and an ordinary of crests are classification systems used in heraldry to organize coats of arms and crests by their elements, such as charges, colors, and arrangements. These ordinaries help users identify and compare heraldic symbols based on their visual characteristics, making it easier to trace connections and study the development of heraldic art.

How can I find out if my family has a coat of arms?

If you suspect your family has a coat of arms, you can start by researching historical records, family documents, or consulting our general armory on Araldika. You may also contact the Office of the Chief Herald of Malta for assistance in verifying or registering a coat of arms.

Contributing and Engaging

Can I create my own coat of arms?

In Malta, the creation of a new coat of arms is regulated by the Office of the Chief Herald. If you are interested in having a coat of arms designed or granted, you should contact the Chief Herald, who can guide you through the process and ensure that the design adheres to heraldic principles and traditions.

How does Araldika contribute to the preservation of Maltese heraldry?

Araldika serves as a digital platform dedicated to the preservation and promotion of Maltese heraldry. By providing access to a general armory, ordinaries, and a directory of mottoes, we make it easier for scholars, enthusiasts, and the general public to explore and appreciate Malta's heraldic heritage. We aim to support ongoing research and education in the field.
